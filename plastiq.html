What‚Äôs a TIN?

A TIN is an umbrella term for several identifiers that an entity may use for tax-reporting purposes. A business may use an employer identification number (EIN), social security number (SSN), or individual taxpayer identification number (ITIN) as their TIN.

Form W9 is used to report TINs

When Texas cited us under 31 CFR ¬ß 1010.410 for failure to collect TINs in 2017, we quickly addressed the issue by using a third party service that would automatically retrieve the required information. Given the chance to reimagine this solution with the launch of our business app, we decided to take a more transparent approach and ask businesses to provide their TINs.

How can we minimize inconvenience?

I spoke to various people at the company with knowledge on the cultural context of TINs to understand how I could minimize the inconvenience of providing TINs.

Testing & Refinement

I ran several tests on usertesting.com to account for any oversights and answer the following research questions:

  • How do users react when they are asked for a TIN after adding a card?
  • Do users understand why we‚Äôre asking for a TIN? What unanswered questions do they have?
  • Do users understand how to add a TIN if they skip it in card addition?

Testing helped uncover important insights about the language used: despite being technically accurate, using the acronym ‚ÄúTIN‚Äù along with ‚ÄúEIN‚Äù and ‚ÄúSSN‚Äù was confusing to users. I opted to use ‚ÄúTax ID‚Äù instead of ‚ÄúTIN‚Äù to add clarity. Multiple users also expressed that they were wary to provide their personal information to an unfamiliar startup, so I added information about our data encryption.
